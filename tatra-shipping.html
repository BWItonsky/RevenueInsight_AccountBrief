8 · Recommended Sales Plays
1
FMC Compliance Upgrade — Customs to Rate Management Bundle
Expansion

Tatra holds an FMC Ocean Freight Forwarder license and operates Hamburg-to-Newark import lanes. They have a legal tariff-filing obligation and active customs workflow — but no Magaya Customs Compliance or Rate Management module. Lead with the compliance angle (risk removal, not feature sell) and bundle both modules into one expansion conversation.

  • Open by referencing the existing Cargo relationship. Call Zuzana: "Hey Zuzana, this is [Rep Name] from Magaya — you've been on Cargo with us for a couple years. I was reviewing your account and noticed you're a licensed NVOCC, which means you're filing FMC tariffs. I want to make sure you know there's a Magaya module built specifically for that — it's actually one of the most popular add-ons for forwarders your size."
  • Qualify the customs workflow. "Right now, when a shipment comes in from Hamburg — how are you handling the customs filing side? Do you have a broker doing that, or is someone on your team self-filing?" The answer dictates whether CC is a cost-displacement or time-savings pitch.
  • Position Magaya Customs Compliance against the specific pain. If outsourcing: "A customs broker for your volume likely runs $200–400 per entry. Magaya CC handles ABI filings inside the same system your cargo is already tracked in — you'd recover the cost on about 10–15 entries and own the process." If self-filing: "If you're self-filing manually, CC automates the ABI submission, CBP response tracking, and billing in one window — eliminates a big slice of administrative time."
  • Layer in Rate Management via the FMC tariff hook. "The other piece — and this might be even more relevant to you as an NVOCC — is that Magaya Rate Management handles your FMC tariff publication directly. You can manage your ocean rates, respond to RFQs, and file your tariff all from one place instead of juggling a separate tariff system."
  • Ask for a 30-minute demo with both Zuzana and Peter. "Can I get 30 minutes with you and Peter to walk through both modules on your actual setup? I can show you exactly how it connects to your existing Cargo workflow — shouldn't take long and you'll know within the first 10 minutes if it's useful."
2
Vehicle Shipping Rate Complexity Play
Expansion

Tatra's vehicle shipping specialty — classic cars, motorcycles, collector vehicles exported from the U.S. to Europe — creates one-off rate and documentation complexity that isn't well-served by a basic Cargo-only workflow. Use the vehicle specialty as the specific pain hook to introduce Magaya Rate Management.

  • Lead with the vehicle-specific pain point. "I was reading through your website — you're doing a lot of classic car and collector vehicle exports, which is a pretty specialized workflow. How are you managing rate quotes for those? Because that's not a standard commodity rate you can just pull off a carrier's rate card."
  • Probe the quoting process. "When a collector contacts you about shipping a vehicle to Slovakia or the Czech Republic, how long does it take to put a quote together? Are you going back to carriers manually each time, or do you have a rate database you're pulling from?"
  • Position Rate Management as the quoting control tower. "Magaya Rate Management lets you store all your carrier rates — ocean, air, specialty cargo surcharges — in one place. When the next classic car inquiry comes in, you're pulling a quote in minutes instead of making three carrier calls. And when rates change, you update once and every quote you run reflects it."
  • Quantify the time savings. "If your team spends 45–60 minutes on each specialty vehicle quote and you're handling even 10–15 of those a month, that's 8–15 hours of quoting time. Rate Management typically cuts that by 60–70%. For a team your size, that's effectively getting a part-time person back."
  • Offer a scoped, low-stakes next step. "Let me send you a quick overview specifically on Rate Management for specialty freight forwarders — two pages, not a brochure. And if it looks relevant, we can do a 20-minute screen share where I show you how it works against a sample vehicle shipment quote. Fair?"
Lead with Play 1 if Zuzana owns the customs workflow. Lead with Play 2 if Peter is the first point of contact and owns the vehicle specialty. Both plays ultimately converge on the same module recommendation — start where the pain is sharpest.

10 · Open Questions for First Call
  1. How are you currently handling FMC tariff filing? Is that done inside Magaya Cargo today, or are you using a separate system or third party to publish your tariff as an NVOCC?
  2. On the customs side — are you self-filing ABI entries for your Hamburg-Newark imports, or are you using an outside customs broker? If outsourcing, roughly how many entries per month?
  3. When a vehicle or specialty shipment inquiry comes in, how are you building the rate quote? How long does that process typically take, and is it centralized or does each person pull rates differently?
  4. You've expanded to 30+ European countries — are you managing all those carrier relationships and rates in one place, or is it spread across spreadsheets, emails, and individual carrier systems?
  5. Your website is clearly growing — bilingual, WhatsApp support, new service lines. Is the plan to keep growing the business, or is it more about running the current volume more efficiently? (This tells you whether to pitch growth enablement or operational efficiency.)
  6. Who is the decision maker on software and tools for the business — do you and Peter decide those together, or does one of you own the operations technology side?
