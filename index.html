3 · Why Amica Is a Fit for TWC
  • Core product lines are inherently weather-driven. Auto, home, and umbrella insurance claims are directly triggered by weather events — hail, ice storms, flooding, windstorms, hurricanes. Amica's risk exposure maps directly to TWC's trigger density capabilities.
  • Active programmatic buyer with $116.7M annual spend (Winmo). Digital $50.9M, Broadcast $31.8M, CTV $12.1M. Confirmed buying on The Trade Desk (TTD), YouTube, and direct display across 250+ properties. PMP infrastructure is already in place — TWC has transacted with them on PMP before.
  • New Merkle data partnership signals audience-first approach. Amica is explicitly investing in first- and third-party data for targeted audience development. TWC's weather audience overlays (by geography, trigger type, and season) are a direct complement to Merkle's identity-based targeting model.
  • Seasonal peaks align perfectly with TWC content moments. Storm prep (spring/fall), hurricane season, winter weather, and freeze events all generate high-attention TWC inventory precisely when Amica needs to reach homeowners and drivers in high-risk moments.
  • Growing appetite for large-scale reach partnerships. The Penn State multiyear sponsorship (Sep 2025) shows Amica is willing to commit budget to scaled audience deals beyond pure performance — exactly the framing TWC's on-property inventory supports.
  • Brand campaign messaging aligns with weather moments. "Empathy is our best policy" — connecting with people in moments of vulnerability — is naturally paired with weather events, which are among the highest emotional attention moments in a consumer's year.
